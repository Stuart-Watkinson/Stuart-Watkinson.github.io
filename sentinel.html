The Sentinel's Escape

Details

Role: Gameplay Designer & Programmer
Duration: 5 Weeks
Team Size: 1
Engine: Unreal Engine [Blueprint]

About

This project was developed as a marked assessment in my second term at University of Staffordshire. The goal of this project was to develop and demonstrate a complete gameplay experience with Unreal Engine 5, with emphasis on the level and mechanics design.

Introduction

Over the course of this projects development I was provided with a crash course over the basics of Unreal Engine 5’s capabilities. This gave a lot of room to spare for exploration/experimentation with these systems, with such freedom I aimed throughout to develop an experience that was truly my own. The use of blueprinting allowed for rapid iteration on systems, due to the system being built for prototyping. This allowed for open discussion with peers and mentors on how systems could be developed with plenty of room being given for trial and error.

Gameplay Design

Overhead of the map

The design of the game was the major focus of the project, using a framework on top of iterative prototyping, making sure there was a sense of completeness with the level and mechanics. To achieve this I worked alongside peers on ideas and play testing. This communication was vital to the eventual product.

A main critique I had throughout the development was play testers feeling lost and confused. This often lead me to going back to the drawing board and taking apart my previous iterations to see why this could be the case, this came down to two main issues: low visibility, and unintuitive room layouts. The low visibility was a mix of lighting and prop placement, one play-tester gave me very in-depth thoughts on the props especially, sitting down and working through the issue with them helped to bring the level to what it is now, props not just being used decoration but also to draw the player to the next destination. Alongside this I added text pop ups when new mechanics were needed to get past a new section, as I found players thought the game broke or was unbeatable due to the mechanics not being intuitive.

What was Learnt

This project was a true crash course over Unreal Engine 5 as a whole. There were many micro discoveries I made throughout, using all the tools I could understand from level sculpting to the niagara system. Though, outside of Unreal Engine itself, my major take away from the project as a whole was how important getting others perspectives is. A sense of tunnel vision is inevitable when working on a project for a long time, this can lead to a disconnect being formed with designing intuitive systems that anyone could understand, and believing “it’s self explanatory”. Being able to put my head on straight with some feedback helped to stay on track and in scope to create a better experience.
